## Article 10 — Repeals and transitory clauses

**1. Platform-to-Business Regulation (EU) 2019/1150 — repealed** with effect from *[date = entry into
application of this Regulation]*.
▸ The proposal **repeals P2B outright**. (The Council later changed this to a **partial amendment**, deleting
only listed articles — see [council Art 10](../council/ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md#article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory).)

**2. Derogation — certain P2B provisions continue to apply until 31 December 2032:** Article 2, point (1);
Article 2, point (2); Article 2, point (5); Article 4; Article 11; Article 15.
▸ These cross-referenced provisions are preserved temporarily for legal certainty (cf. recital 59, citing
e.g. Directive (EU) 2023/2831 on platform work). The Council **struck this derogation**.

**3. The following acts are repealed**, with effect from *[date, aligned with the entry into application of
the amendments]*:
- **Regulation (EU) 2022/868** — Data Governance Act (DGA);
- **Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** — Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation (FFNPD);
- **Directive 2019/1024** — Open Data Directive.

**4. Correlation.** References to Regulation (EU) 2022/868, Regulation (EU) 2018/1807 and Directive 2019/1024
shall be read in accordance with the **correlation table set out in Annex I** of this Regulation.
▸ The substance of these acts is relocated, principally into the **Data Act (EU) 2023/2854** via
[Article 1](COM-2025-837_dataact-art1.md). Annex I is committed separately at
`../../sources/commission/COM-2025-837_annex1-correlation-tables_2025-11-19.docx`.
See repealed-instruments page.

## Article 11 — Final provisions

*"This Regulation shall enter into force on the **third day** following that of its publication in the
Official Journal of the European Union."*

Staggered application (bracketed/derogating dates as in the source):

| Provisions | Apply from |
|---|---|
| General rule (most of the Regulation) | entry into force (3rd day after publication) |
| ePrivacy Article 5(3) carve-out (via Article 5(2) of this Regulation) | **6 months** after publication |
| Article 3(8) points (a)–(c) (breach-notification routing), Articles 6(2)–(3) and 7 to 9 (cyber) | **18 months** from entry into force |
| Reporting **via the single-entry point**, where the Commission's Art 23a(7) assessment is negative | **24 months** from entry into force |

▸ Note: the GDPR Art 88a (6 months) and Art 88b (24/48 months) application dates are set inside those
articles themselves — see
[GDPR Art 3 point 15](COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent).
The Council later moved the single-entry-point **reporting** switch-over from **24 → 30 months**.

*"This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States."*
