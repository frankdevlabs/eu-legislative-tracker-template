> **Source:** Commission proposal **COM(2025) 837 final**, 19 November 2025 — *Digital Omnibus*.
> File 2025/0360 (COD). **Working transcription, not an official text** — the proposal's own amending

**Why this file exists:** Regulation (EU) 2018/1725 governs data protection by the **EU institutions, bodies,
offices and agencies**. The proposal **mirrors its GDPR (Article 3) amendments here** so the two regimes stay
homogeneous (recitals 41–42). There is **no Council counterpart** under `../council/` — the Council extracts
do not transcribe the EUDPR article — so this file has no diff sibling; read it alongside
[`COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md`](COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md).

*Regulation (EU) 2018/1725 is amended as follows:*

---

## Point 1 — Article 3 (definitions)

**(a) Addition to Art 3(1) (concept of personal data):** the same three sentences added to GDPR Art 4(1) are
added here (information is not necessarily personal for every entity; not personal where the entity cannot
identify by means reasonably likely to be used by it; not personal merely because a subsequent recipient
could identify).

**(b) Point (25) replaced:** *"for 'electronic communications networks' the definition of Article 2(1) of
Directive (EU) 2018/1972 shall apply"*.

**(c) New definitions added:** **(27) 'mobile application'** (Art 3(2) of Directive (EU) 2016/2102);
**(28) 'online interface'** (Art 3(m) of Regulation (EU) 2022/2065); **(29) 'scientific research'** — same
definition as GDPR Art 4(38).

## Point 2 — Article 4(1)(b) (purpose limitation)
Replaced to mirror GDPR Art 5(1)(b): research/archiving/statistics further processing compatible (per
Article 13), independent of the Article 6 conditions.

## Point 3 — Article 10 (special categories)
New Art 10(2) points **(k)** (AI development/operation) and **(l)** (biometric verification), and a new
**paragraph 4** mirroring GDPR Art 9(5) (avoid/remove residual special-category data in AI datasets).

## Point 4 — Article 14(5) (abusive requests)
Replaced to mirror GDPR Art 12(5): free-of-charge information; on manifestly unfounded/excessive or abusive
Art 17 requests the controller may **refuse to act** (note: no "reasonable fee" option here); controller
bears the burden of proof.

## Point 5 — Article 15(5) (research exemption)
New paragraph mirroring GDPR Art 13(5): no duty to provide information where research processing makes it
impossible or a disproportionate effort, subject to Article 13 safeguards.

## Point 6 — Article 24 (automated decisions)
Art 24(1) and (2) replaced to mirror GDPR Art 22, including the **"regardless of whether the decision could
be taken otherwise than by solely automated means"** clause in (a).

## Point 7 — Article 34(1) (breach notification)
Replaced: high-risk-only threshold; **96 hours**; notify the **European Data Protection Supervisor** (the
EUDPR's supervisor — not, here, via the single-entry point).

## Point 8 — Article 37 (terminal-equipment processing & machine-readable signals)
New paragraphs added to Article 37 mirroring **GDPR Articles 88a/88b**: consent to store/access personal data
in terminal equipment (new (2)), the Union-law carve-out (new (3)), the limitative list of consent-exempt
purposes (new (4)), the consent conditions incl. single-click refusal and 6-month no-repeat (new (5)),
application from *[OP date = 6 months …]* (new (6)), automated/machine-readable consent interfaces (new (7)),
the duty to respect those choices (new (8)), the conformity presumption referencing GDPR Art 88b(4) (new (9)),
and application of (7)–(9) from *[OP date = 24 months …]* (new (10)).

## Point (8 bis) — Article 39 (DPIA alignment)
Art 39(4) replaced so the DPIA lists/template/methodology adopted under GDPR Art 35(6a) apply to EU-institution
processing; Art 39(5) and (6) deleted.

## Point (9) — new Article 45a (pseudonymisation alignment)
*"The common criteria adopted by the Commission and referred to in article 41a of the Regulation (EU) 2016/679
should apply to the processing of personal data under this Regulation."*

---

▸ Throughout, Article 4 is the **EU-institutions mirror** of [GDPR Article 3](COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md);
the salient design choices (relative personal-data clause, AI legitimate interest, terminal-equipment consent,
Commission-led implementing powers) are the same, and were later reworked or dropped by the Council in the GDPR
text — the EUDPR article would follow in a later alignment exercise (recital 43).
