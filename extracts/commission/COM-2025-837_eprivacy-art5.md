# Extract — COM(2025) 837, Article 5 (amendments to the ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC)

> **Source:** Commission proposal **COM(2025) 837 final**, 19 November 2025 — *Digital Omnibus*.
> File 2025/0360 (COD). **Working transcription, not an official text** — the proposal's own amending
> instructions, transcribed verbatim. Verify against the authoritative document (EUR-Lex **CELEX
> 52025PC0837**). See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**Why this file is short in the proposal:** the Commission proposed to move the cookie / terminal-equipment
consent regime **out of the ePrivacy Directive and into the GDPR** (new
[Article 88a](COM-2025-837_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent)). So the
proposal only **deletes** ePrivacy Article 4 and adds a carve-out to Article 5(3). The Council later
**reversed this design**, deleting GDPR Art 88a and instead **rewriting ePrivacy Art 5(3)** to keep the
consent rule here — diff against [`../council/ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md`](../council/ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md).

*Directive 2002/58/EC is amended as follows:*

---

## Point 1 — Article 4 deleted

**Article 4 is deleted.**
▸ Article 4 (security of processing; personal-data-breach notification for electronic communications
providers) is removed; per recital 48 the subject matter is covered by the **GDPR** (Arts 32–34) and the
**NIS2 Directive** (security and incident reporting). The Council also deletes Article 4.

---

<a id="article-5-3-terminal-equipment-consent"></a>
## Point 2 — Article 5(3), new subparagraph added

After Article 5(3), the following subparagraph is added:
*"This paragraph shall not apply if the subscriber or user is a natural person, and the information stored or
accessed constitutes or leads to the processing of personal data."*
▸ This carve-out **disapplies the ePrivacy Art 5(3) consent rule** for natural persons whenever the stored/
accessed information is (or leads to) personal data — because in that case the new **GDPR Article 88a** would
govern instead. ePrivacy Art 5(3) thus survives in the proposal only for **non-natural-person** subscribers
/ users and non-personal information (cf. recital 47).
▸ **Contrast with the Council:** ST 9547/26 keeps GDPR Art 88a deleted and instead **replaces ePrivacy
Art 5(3) outright** with a full consent rule (single-click refusal; consent-exempt purposes incl. anonymous
first-party audience measurement; 6-month no-repeat; GDPR supervisory authority as enforcer) and adds a new
Article 17 transposition paragraph — see [council Art 5](../council/ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md).
