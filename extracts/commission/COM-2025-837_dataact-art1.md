# Extract — COM(2025) 837, Article 1 (amendments to the Data Act, Reg. (EU) 2023/2854)

> **Source:** Commission proposal **COM(2025) 837 final**, 19 November 2025 — *Digital Omnibus*.
> File 2025/0360 (COD). **Working transcription, not an official text** — the proposal's own amending
> instructions and inserted operative text. Verify against the authoritative document (EUR-Lex **CELEX
> 52025PC0837**). See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**This is the largest article in the proposal.** Article 1 turns the **Data Act** (Reg (EU) 2023/2854) into
the single consolidated instrument for the EU data economy: it (a) calibrates the existing Data Act (trade
secrets, business-to-government, switching, smart contracts) and (b) **absorbs** the **Data Governance Act**
(Reg (EU) 2022/868), the **Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation** (Reg (EU) 2018/1807) and the **Open
Data Directive** (Dir 2019/1024) — which Article 10 then repeals (see
[final provisions](COM-2025-837_final-art10-11.md#article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory) and
repealed-instruments page).

There is **no Council counterpart** under `../council/` for this article (the Council extracts cover only the
GDPR/ePrivacy/cyber/final strands). Because of its length, the inserted chapters below are transcribed at the
level of each new Article's number, heading and operative substance; quoted passages reproduce the proposal's
wording for the load-bearing provisions.

*Regulation (EU) 2023/2854 is amended as follows:*

---

## Calibrating the existing Data Act (points 1–17)

**1. Article 1 (subject matter and scope)** — new points inserted in Art 1(1): **(ea)** voluntary
registration of data intermediation services; **(eb)** voluntary registration of data-altruism entities;
**(ec)** establishment of a European Data Innovation Board; **(ed)** data-localisation requirements and
availability of data to competent authorities; **(ee)** re-use of certain public-sector data/documents and
research data. New points added to Art 1(2) on which data each new Chapter covers (VIIa: personal and
non-personal; VIIb: non-personal; VIIc: documents/research data/protected data per Art 32i). Art 1(3)(g)
replaced ("participants in data spaces"); Art 1(7) deleted; new paragraphs 11–13 on the relationship with
sector-specific and Member-State law.

**2. Article 2 (definitions)** — extensive new/replaced definitions, including: **(4a)** 'consent' (= GDPR
Art 4(11)); **(4b)** 'permission' (right to process non-personal data); **(4c)** 'access'; **(13)** 'data
holder' replaced; **(28a)** 'bodies governed by public law'; **(28b)** 'public undertaking'; **(38a)** 'data
intermediation service' and **(38b)** 'data altruism' (relocated and refined from the DGA); and a block of
definitions **(44)–(63)** carrying over Open Data Directive / DGA concepts ('medium-sized enterprise',
'small mid-cap', 'university', 'standard licence', 'document', 'dynamic data', 'research data', 're-use',
'high-value datasets', 'certain categories of protected data', 'secure processing environment', 're-user',
'machine-readable format', 'open format', 'formal open standard', 'reasonable return on investment', 'data
localisation requirement', 'pseudonymisation' (= GDPR Art 4(5))).

**3. Article 4(8) replaced — trade-secret refusal (data holder ↔ user).** Adds a new refusal ground: a data
holder that is a trade-secret holder may, case-by-case, refuse access where it demonstrates *"a high risk of
unlawful acquisition, use, or disclosure to third country entities, or entities established in the Union under
the direct or indirect control of such entities, which are subject to jurisdictions offering weaker or
non-equivalent protection compared to that under Union law"*, with a duly substantiated written demonstration
and notification of the competent authority (Art 37).

**4. Article 5(11) replaced** — the same new trade-secret refusal ground for the data holder ↔ **third party**
relationship.

**5. Title of Chapter V replaced:** *"MAKING DATA AVAILABLE TO PUBLIC SECTOR BODIES, THE COMMISSION, THE
EUROPEAN CENTRAL BANK AND UNION BODIES ON THE BASIS OF A PUBLIC EMERGENCY"*.
▸ Narrows the business-to-government chapter from *"exceptional need"* to **"public emergencies"** (recital 15).

**6. Articles 14 and 15 deleted** (the old "exceptional need" provisions).

**7. New Article 15a** — *Obligation for data holders to make data available on the basis of a public
emergency.* A public sector body, the Commission, the ECB or a Union body with an exceptional need to respond
to / mitigate / recover from a **public emergency** may request data from data holders that are legal persons;
requests for response concern **non-personal data** (personal data only where non-personal is insufficient,
pseudonymised where possible); mitigation/recovery requests concern non-personal data only and **not** from
micro/small enterprises.

**8. Article 16(2) replaced** — Chapter V does not apply to law-enforcement/criminal/administrative-offence,
customs or taxation activities.

**9. Article 17** — references updated to Article 15a; Art 17(1) and (2) introductory wording and points
amended; Art 17(2)(e) deleted; Art 17(5) and (6) deleted.

**10. Article 18** — Art 18(2) introductory wording amended (decline/modify timelines: 5 working days for
Art 15a(2) requests, 30 working days for Art 15a(3)); Art 18(5) deleted.

**11. Article 19** — references updated to Article 15a; Art 19(3) (trade-secret disclosure to public bodies)
replaced.

**12. Article 20 replaced — Compensation for making data available under Chapter V.** Data for **response** to
a public emergency (Art 15a(2)) is provided **free of charge** (with public acknowledgement on request); data
for **mitigation/recovery** (Art 15a(3)) carries **fair compensation** (costs + reasonable margin);
**micro/small enterprises** may claim compensation even for response data; no compensation for official-
statistics production where national law bars purchase.

**13. Article 21 amended** — heading replaced ("Sharing of data obtained in the context of a public emergency
with research organisations or statistical bodies"); Art 21(5) replaced with notification duties to the data
holder.

**14. New Article 22a (inserted before Chapter VI) — Right to lodge a complaint** with the competent authority
(Art 37) where a dispute arises over an Article 15a request, refusal, modification, compensation level, or
onward transmission.

**15. Article 31 — new paragraphs 1a and 1b (switching / custom-made & SME/SMC services).** Carves the
Chapter VI switching obligations (except Art 29) and Art 34 out of (i) data-processing services **adapted to
the specific needs of the customer** and (ii) services provided by **SMEs/SMCs**, where based on a contract
concluded **before or on 12 September 2025**; no duty to renegotiate before expiry; contractual terms contrary
to Art 29(1)–(3) are null and void; proportionate early-termination penalties allowed in fixed-duration
contracts (recitals 17–18).

**16. Article 32 amended** — third-country governmental-access safeguards (Art 32(1)–(5)) extended to public
sector bodies / re-users / data-intermediation and data-altruism entities under the new chapters.

**17. Article 36 deleted** — removes the **smart-contracts** essential-requirements provision (recital 16).

## Inserted chapters (point 18)

**18. The following Chapters VIIa, VIIb and VIIc are inserted.**

### CHAPTER VIIa — Data intermediation services and data altruism organisations
*(relocates and streamlines the DGA regime; voluntary registration.)*
- **Article 32a — Public Union registers.** The Commission keeps Union registers of recognised data
  intermediation services providers and recognised data altruism organisations; entry confers the right to use
  the respective **label** and **common logo** (Commission implementing acts on logo design).
- **Article 32b — Competent authorities for registration**, set up to be independent of any registered entity.
- **Article 32c — General requirements for registration of data intermediation services providers** (data used
  only to put it at users' disposal; activity data used only to develop the service; additional tools only at
  the user's request; value-added-service conditions incl. functional separation and a gatekeeper exclusion;
  best-interest duty toward data subjects).
- **Article 32d — General requirements for registration of data altruism organisations** (data-altruism
  activities; legal person of general interest; not-for-profit and independent; functional separation).
- **Article 32e — Registration** (application to the main-establishment competent authority; non-EU entities
  designate a legal representative; 12-week decision; Union-wide validity; proportionate, waivable fees).
- **Article 32f — Duties of recognised data altruism organisations** (prior information; purpose limitation; no
  misleading marketing; electronic consent/withdrawal; breach notification; third-country specification).
- **Article 32g — Monitoring of compliance** (supervision; information powers; cure period; loss of label and
  removal from the register on persistent non-compliance).

### CHAPTER VIIb — Free flow of non-personal data in the Union
*(relocates the surviving FFNPD principle.)*
- **Article 32h — Prohibition of localisation requirements for non-personal data.** *"Data localisation
  requirements for non-personal data shall be prohibited, unless they are justified on grounds of **public
  security** in compliance with the principle of proportionality or laid down on the basis of Union law."*
  Member States must notify draft localisation measures to the Commission (Dir (EU) 2015/1535 procedure).

### CHAPTER VIIc — Re-use of data and documents held by public sector bodies
*(merges the Open Data Directive with DGA Chapter II.)*
- **Section 1 — General provisions:** **32i** subject matter and scope (public-sector data/documents incl.
  protected data; certain public-undertaking data; research data; with detailed exclusions and IP/database-
  right safeguards); **32j** non-discrimination; **32k** exclusive arrangements (prohibited save defined
  general-interest/cultural-digitisation derogations, with transition deadlines); **32l** general charging
  principles; **32m** information on means of redress.
- **Section 2 — Re-use of open government data:** **32n** general principle; **32o** processing requests for
  re-use (timelines; redress); **32p** available formats ("open by design and by default"; dynamic data via
  APIs; high-value datasets); **32q** charging principles (free of charge save marginal costs; exceptions;
  **higher charges for very large enterprises / gatekeepers**); **32r** standard licences (incl. special
  conditions for very large enterprises); **32s** practical arrangements; **32t** research data ("open by
  default", FAIR); **32u** thematic categories of **high-value datasets** (Annex I; delegated acts); **32v**
  specific high-value datasets and arrangements for publication/re-use (implementing acts; free of charge,
  machine-readable, via APIs/bulk download, with derogations).
- **Section 3 — Re-use of certain categories of protected data held by public sector bodies:** **32w**
  conditions for re-use (preserve protected nature; anonymisation / secure processing environment;
  consent/permission where needed); **32x** requirements for transfers of non-personal data to third countries
  by re-users (model clauses; third-country adequacy-style declarations); **32y** fees (cost-based; incentives
  for SMEs/SMCs and research; higher fees for very large enterprises); **32z** competent bodies (assistance,
  secure environments); **32aa** single information point (national; European single access point); **32ab**
  procedure for requests for re-use (two-month decision; redress).

## Governance, delegation, committee, review (points 19–27)

**19. Article 38 replaced** — right to lodge a complaint (incl. against registered data-intermediation /
data-altruism entities) with the relevant competent authority.

**20. Article 40 — new paragraph 6:** Article 40 does not apply to Chapter VIIc.

**21. New heading before Article 41 inserted: 'CHAPTER IXa — European Data Innovation Board'.**

**22. New Article 41a — European Data Innovation Board (EDIB)** established to advise/assist the Commission and
serve as a forum for the European data economy (composition incl. Member States, the new competent
authorities, EDPB, EDPS, ENISA, the SME Envoy; Commission-chaired).

**23. Article 42 replaced — Role of the EDIB** (strategic forum; consistent enforcement of Chapters II, III,
V, VII, VIIa and VIIc; cooperation and good-practice exchange).

**24. Article 45 amended** — delegated-acts provisions (Art 45(2), (3), (6)) updated to reference Art 29(7),
Art 32u(2) and Art 33(2).

**25. Article 46 amended** — committee procedure (Art 46(1) and new (1a) referencing Reg (EU) No 182/2011).

**26. Article 49 amended** — evaluation provisions: Art 49(1) re-scoped (evaluation of Chapters II–VIII by
12 September 2028; point (m) on SMEs/SMCs replaced); new **Art 49(2a)** requiring a separate evaluation of the
new Chapters VIIa, VIIb and VIIc within five years; Art 49(5) updated.

**27. Annex I is added** as set out in **Annex II** to this Regulation (the high-value-dataset thematic
categories table).

---

▸ **Net effect:** the Data Act becomes the single home for (i) the calibrated original Data Act rules, (ii) the
DGA's data-intermediation and data-altruism regimes (now **voluntary**), (iii) the FFNPD non-personal-data
free-flow principle, and (iv) the merged Open Data Directive / DGA public-sector re-use rules. The three
absorbed acts are repealed by [Article 10](COM-2025-837_final-art10-11.md#article-10-amendments-repeals-transitory),
with a correlation table in **Annex I**
(committed source).
