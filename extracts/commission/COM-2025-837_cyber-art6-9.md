This is the **single-entry-point** architecture for incident reporting in the original proposal. The
proposal builds the single entry point on **ENISA** as one combined facility (new NIS2 **Article 23a**); the
Council later split it into an ENISA *information point* (Art 23a) plus Member-State *national entry points*
(Art 23b). Diff against [`../council/ST-9547-2026_cyber-art6-9.md`](../council/ST-9547-2026_cyber-art6-9.md).

## Article 6 — Amendments to the NIS2 Directive (EU) 2022/2555

**1. New Article 23a — Single-entry point for incident reporting.**
- (1) **ENISA shall develop and maintain a single-entry point** to support the obligation to report
  incidents and related events under Union legal acts where those acts so provide. ENISA may build it on the
  single reporting platform established under the **Cyber Resilience Act** (Reg (EU) 2024/2847), without
  prejudice to its Article 16.
- (2) ENISA shall take appropriate technical, operational and organisational **security measures** for the
  single-entry point and the information submitted/disseminated, taking account of sensitivity and ensuring
  competent authorities under the relevant acts have access as required.
- (3) ENISA shall provide and implement specifications (developed with the Commission, the CSIRTs network and
  the competent authorities) ensuring: (a) interoperability with other reporting obligations; (b) technical
  arrangements for entities/authorities to access, submit, retrieve, transmit and further process
  information; (c) the specificities of each act's reporting requirements are taken into account; (d) where
  relevant, interoperability with **European Business Wallets** (usable at least to identify/authenticate
  entities); (e) entities can retrieve and supplement previously submitted information; (f) **a single
  notification can be used to fulfil reporting obligations under any of the other Union acts** that provide
  for reporting to the single-entry point.
- (4) Unless an act provides otherwise, **ENISA shall not have access** to the notifications submitted.
- (5) Within **[18] months** from entry into force, ENISA shall **pilot** the functioning for each added
  Union legal act; notifications are enabled only after piloting and after the Commission publishes a notice
  under paragraph 6.
- (6) The Commission, with ENISA, shall **assess** proper functioning, reliability, integrity and
  confidentiality and, if satisfied (after consulting the CSIRTs network and competent authorities),
  **publish a notice in the Official Journal**.
- (7) If the assessment is negative, ENISA shall take corrective measures and the Commission shall reassess
  and publish a notice under paragraph 6.

**2. Article 23 amended:** Art 23(1) first sentence replaced so that essential/important entities notify
significant incidents **via the single-entry point** established under Article 23a; new Art 23(12) lets a
manufacturer's severe-incident report under Art 14(3) of Reg (EU) 2024/2847 (CRA) **count as** the NIS2
Art 23(4) report where it contains the required information.

**3. Article 30 amended:** Art 30(1) replaced so that **voluntary** notifications (incidents, cyber threats,
near misses) can also be submitted **via the single-entry point**.

▸ The Council later renames the routing channel to a **national entry point (Art 23b)** with the ENISA
facility as an information/redirection point (Art 23a), and adds harmonisation / cross-border articles
(council Arts 23c, 23d).

## Article 7 — Amendments to the eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014

- **Article 19a — new paragraph (1a):** notifications under Art 19a(1)(b) to the supervisory body and other
  relevant competent authorities shall be made **through the single-entry point** (Art 23a NIS2).
- **Article 24 — new paragraph (2a):** notifications under Art 24(2)(fb) to the supervisory body and other
  relevant competent bodies shall be made through the single-entry point.
- **Article 45a — new paragraph (3a):** notifications under Art 45a(3) to the Commission and the competent
  supervisory body shall be made through the single-entry point.

---

## Article 8 — Amendments to DORA, Regulation (EU) 2022/2554

Article 19 is amended:
- **Article 19(1)** first subparagraph replaced: financial entities report **major ICT-related incidents** to
  the relevant competent authority (Art 46) **via the single-entry point** (Art 23a NIS2), per Art 19(4).
- **Article 19(2)** first subparagraph replaced: financial entities may voluntarily notify **significant
  cyber threats** via the single-entry point.
▸ The change is the **channel**, not the substance — DORA's own timelines and templates continue to apply.

---

## Article 9 — Amendments to the CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557

Article 15 is amended:
- **Article 15(1)** first sentence replaced: Member States ensure critical entities notify, via the
  **single-entry point** (Art 23a NIS2), the competent authority without undue delay of incidents that
  significantly disrupt (or could) the provision of essential services.
- **Article 15(2)** new subparagraph: the Commission may adopt **implementing acts** specifying the type and
  format of information notified under Art 15(1) (examination procedure, Art 24(2)).

---

**Application dates** (see [final provisions](COM-2025-837_final-art10-11.md)): under the proposal, Art 3(8)
points (a)–(c), Articles 6(2)–(3) and 7 to 9 apply **18 months** from entry into force; if the Commission's
Art 23a(7) assessment is negative, the obligations to report via the single-entry point apply **24 months**
from entry into force. (The Council later moved the reporting switch-over to **30 months**.)
