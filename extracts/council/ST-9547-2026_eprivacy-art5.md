# Extract — ST 9547/26, Article 5 (amendments to the ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC)

> **Source:** Council document **ST 9547/26** (LIMITE), 21 May 2026 — Presidency revised compromise text,
> file 2025/0360 (COD). **Working transcription, not an official text** — consolidated reading (tracked
> changes accepted). Verify against the authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

**Why this file matters:** because the Council **deleted GDPR Article 88a**
([see GDPR extract](ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-15--articles-88-machine-readable-consent)),
the cookie / terminal-equipment **consent regime stays here**, in an amended ePrivacy Directive.

*Directive 2002/58/EC is amended as follows:*

---

## Point 1 — Article 4 deleted

Article 4 (security of processing; breach provisions) is **deleted** — the subject matter is covered by the
GDPR (Articles 32–34) and NIS2.

---

<a id="article-5-3-terminal-equipment-consent"></a>
## Point 2 — Article 5(3) replaced (terminal-equipment consent)

*"3. Member States shall ensure that the storing of information, or the gaining of access to information
already stored, in the terminal equipment of a subscriber or user is only allowed when that person has
given his or her **consent**, in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679.*

*Storing of information, or gaining of access to information already stored, in the terminal equipment of a
natural person without consent, and the subsequent processing for the same purpose, shall be lawful to the
extent it is **strictly necessary** for any of the following purposes:*

- *(a) carrying out the transmission of an electronic communication over an electronic communications
  network;*
- *(b) providing a service explicitly requested by the user;*
- *(c) creating **anonymous aggregated** information about the usage of an online service requested by the
  user, to **measure the audience** of such a service, where it is carried out by the provider of that
  online service, or by a third party acting together with or on behalf of that provider, solely for their
  own use, including where the third party is performing audience measurement in accordance with Article 24
  of Regulation (EU) 2024/1083;*
- *(d) maintaining or restoring the security — including in particular cybersecurity, the protection of
  personal data and the privacy of the user, and the prevention of fraud — of the interface strictly
  necessary for the provision of an information society service requested by the user, or of the terminal
  equipment used for the provision of such service.*

*The user shall be able to **refuse** requests for consent in an easy and intelligible manner, with a
**single-click button or equivalent means**.*

*If the user gives consent, the provider shall not make a new request for consent for the same purpose for
the period during which the controller can lawfully rely on the consent of the data subject. If the data
subject **refuses** a request for consent, the controller shall not make a new request for consent for the
same purpose for a period of **at least six months**.*

*Member States shall designate the competent supervisory authority under Regulation (EU) 2016/679 for the
supervision and enforcement of the rules under this paragraph."*

▸ Key features: consent anchored to the GDPR; a closed list of consent-exempt strictly-necessary purposes
(transmission; explicitly-requested service; **anonymous first-party audience measurement**; security incl.
fraud prevention); **single-click refusal**; **6-month** no-repeat after refusal; and enforcement by the
**GDPR supervisory authority** (in the Netherlands, the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens) rather than the
telecoms regulator.

---

## Point 2a — Article 17 new paragraph (transposition)

Added: *"3. Member States shall adopt and publish, by [24 months after the adoption of this Regulation], the
laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary to comply with Article 5(3). They shall apply
those measures from [the same date]."*
