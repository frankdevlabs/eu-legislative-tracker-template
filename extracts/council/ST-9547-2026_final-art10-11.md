## Article 10 — Amendments, repeals and transitory provisions

**1. Platform-to-Business Regulation (EU) 2019/1150 — partially amended (not repealed).**
The following provisions are **deleted** as of [date of entry into force / application]: Articles **2(11)**,
**2(12)**, **6**, **8 to 10**, **12 to 14**, and **16 to 18**.
▸ Earlier reporting described P2B as *repealed*; the May text instead performs a **partial amendment**,
deleting the listed articles while leaving the rest of the Regulation in force. The Commission's proposed
transitional derogation ("by way of derogation … continue to apply until 31 December 2032") is **struck out**.

**3. The following acts are repealed** with effect from [date]:
- **(a) Regulation (EU) 2022/868** — Data Governance Act (DGA);
- **(b) Regulation (EU) 2018/1807** — Free Flow of Non-Personal Data Regulation (FFNPD);
- **(c) Directive (EU) 2019/1024** — Open Data Directive.

**4. Correlation.** References to the repealed acts shall be construed as references to the corresponding
provisions (as relocated, principally into the Data Act (EU) 2023/2854) and read in accordance with the
**correlation table in Annex I**.
▸ Annex I (correlation table) is not part of this extract.

See repealed-instruments page.

## Article 11 — Entry into force and application

*"This Regulation shall enter into force on the **third day** following that of its publication in the
Official Journal of the European Union."*

Staggered application (consolidated, bracketed dates as in the source):

| Provisions | Apply from |
|---|---|
| General rule (most of the Regulation) | date of entry into force |
| The chapter inserting the machine-readable consent / online-interface duties | **18 months** after publication |
| ePrivacy Article 5(3) consent rule (via Art 5(2) of this Regulation) | **6 months** after [entry into force] |
| Article 3 points 8(a)–(c) (breach notification routing) | **18 months** after entry into force |
| Articles 6(2)–(3), 7, 8 and 9 (cyber institutional build-out) | **18 months** after entry into force |
| Incident / breach reporting **via the national entry point** | **30 months** after entry into force |

▸ The national-entry-point **reporting** switch-over moved from **24 → 30 months**. Until the entry point
exists, controllers notify the competent supervisory authority directly
([Art 33(1a)](ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-8--article-33-breach-notification)).

*"This Regulation shall be binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all Member States."*
