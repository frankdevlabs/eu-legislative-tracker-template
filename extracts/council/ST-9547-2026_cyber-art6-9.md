## Article 6 — Amendments to the NIS2 Directive (EU) 2022/2555

**New Article 23a — Incident-reporting information point (ENISA).**
ENISA shall establish and maintain a single **information point** that helps entities identify the
notification obligations applicable to an incident across Union law and **redirects** them to the relevant
national entry point(s). The information point **does not receive or store** the notifications themselves;
it provides information, machine-readable mappings of obligations, and technical support for interoperability.

**New Article 23b — National entry points (Member States).**
Each Member State shall designate or establish one or more **national entry points** through which entities
submit the notifications required under this Directive and under the other Union acts referred to in this
Regulation. National entry points shall be **interoperable** with each other and with the ENISA information
point, shall acknowledge receipt, and shall route each notification to the competent authority/authorities.
Where a national entry point is **technically unavailable**, entities may notify by alternative means and
complete the submission once the entry point is available.

**New Article 23c — Harmonising the incident-notification framework.**
The Commission, supported by ENISA and the Cooperation Group, shall develop a **common framework** (formats,
templates, taxonomies, timelines mapping) to enable a "report once, share with the relevant authorities
many" approach; the Commission may adopt implementing acts specifying technical and procedural requirements.

**New Article 23d — Cross-border and cross-sector notification.**
Where an incident is relevant to authorities in more than one Member State or under more than one Union act,
the national entry point shall ensure the notification is shared with all relevant authorities without undue
delay, respecting confidentiality and security of the information.

**Article 23 (significant incidents) and Article 30** are amended so that the notifications are submitted
**via the national entry point** established under Article 23b.

## Article 7 — Amendments to the eIDAS Regulation (EU) 910/2014

- **Article 19a — new paragraph (1a):** notifications of breaches of security or loss of integrity by
  trust-service-related parties are submitted **via the national entry point** (Art 23b NIS2).
- **Article 24 — new paragraph (2a):** qualified trust service providers' notifications submitted via the
  national entry point.
- **Article 45a — new paragraph (3a):** notifications relating to electronic-attestation/wallet incidents
  submitted via the national entry point.

---

## Article 8 — Amendments to DORA, Regulation (EU) 2022/2554

- **Article 19(1):** financial entities report **major ICT-related incidents** to the competent authority
  **via the national entry point** (Art 23b NIS2).
- **Article 19(2):** voluntary notification of **significant cyber threats** likewise via the national entry
  point.

▸ Operationally relevant for **financial entities** — note DORA's own (stricter) timelines and templates
continue to apply; the change is the **channel**, not the substance.

---

## Article 9 — Amendments to the CER Directive (EU) 2022/2557

- **Article 15(1):** notifications of incidents that significantly disrupt the provision of essential
  services are made **via the national entry point** (Art 23b NIS2).
- **Article 15 — new paragraph:** the Commission may adopt implementing acts specifying the **format and
  procedure** for such notifications.

**Application dates** (see [final provisions](ST-9547-2026_final-art10-11.md)): the entry-point reporting
