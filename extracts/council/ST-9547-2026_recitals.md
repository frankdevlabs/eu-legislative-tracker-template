# Extract — ST 9547/26, key recitals (curated subset)

> **Source:** Council document **ST 9547/26** (LIMITE), 21 May 2026 — Presidency revised compromise text,
> file 2025/0360 (COD). **Working transcription, not an official text** — consolidated reading.
> **This is a curated subset** of the recitals (the ones that carry the substantive choices tracked in this
> repo), not the full preamble. Verify against the authoritative document. See [`../../NOTICE`](../../NOTICE).

Recitals are interpretive, not operative. They matter here because, in the May text, the Council **moved
several substantive ideas out of the articles and into (or out of) recitals** — most notably the AI
legitimate-interest statement.

---

## Recital 27 (concept of personal data)
Retained, pointing back to the existing **Recital 26** and the "means reasonably likely to be used" test;
the Commission's reframing toward a relative/controller-centric concept is **not** carried into an operative
article (Art 4(1) addition deleted — see
[GDPR extract, point 1](ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-1--article-4-definitions)).

## Recital 27a / 27b (pseudonymisation, singling out)
Retained in reworked form; support the new **Article 29a** EDPB-opinion mechanism and the "singling out"
factor in identifiability, rather than a Commission implementing power.

## Recital 30 — [DELETED in ST 9547/26]
The Commission's recital framing AI development/operation as a benefit to society justifying legitimate
interest is **struck out**.

## Recital 31 — [DELETED in ST 9547/26]
The Commission's recital on the **balancing test** for AI legitimate interest is **struck out**.

## Recital 33 (legitimate interest, general)
Retained — general guidance on Article 6(1)(f) balancing.

## Recital 33a — AI and legitimate interest (RETAINED; carries what Art 88c used to)

Consolidated sense: *the processing of personal data in the context of the **development and deployment of
an AI system or model** may be regarded as carried out for a **legitimate interest** of the controller
within the meaning of Article 6(1)(f), provided that the interest is not overridden by the interests or
fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject — in particular where the data subject is a **child** —
and except where Union or national law explicitly requires consent or another legal basis. Appropriate
safeguards should apply, including **data minimisation**, measures to prevent the disclosure or
regurgitation of personal data in outputs, **transparency**, and the effective exercise of the **right to
object** under Article 21.*

▸ This recital is what remains of the deleted **Article 88c**. It is **interpretive only** — there is no
longer a standalone operative AI legitimate-interest article. Practically, AI development on personal data
relies on **ordinary Article 6(1)(f)** read with EDPB **Opinion 28/2024**, not on a bespoke provision.
See AI LI provision.

---

## Recital 38 — [DELETED in ST 9547/26]
The Commission's recital supporting the rewrite of **Article 22** (automated decisions) — including the
"regardless of whether the decision could be taken otherwise than by solely automated means" rationale — is
**struck out**, consistent with Article 22 being largely restored
([GDPR extract, point 7](ST-9547-2026_gdpr-art3-amendments.md#point-7--article-22-automated-decisions)).

## Recital 39 / 39a (cookies / terminal equipment)
Reworked to support keeping the consent rule in the **ePrivacy Directive** (amended Art 5(3)), the
consent-exempt purposes (incl. **anonymous first-party audience measurement** and security/fraud
prevention), and the **6-month** anti-consent-fatigue rule.
See [ePrivacy extract](ST-9547-2026_eprivacy-art5.md).

## Recital 44 / 45 (machine-readable consent signals)
Support the retained machine-readable-signal article and the **browser/OS** technical-means duty; staged
application.

## Recital 46 (single-entry point)
Supports the ENISA information point + national entry points and the "report once" objective.
